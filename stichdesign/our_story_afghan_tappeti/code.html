Chapter One

Our Heritage

For three generations, the Afghan Tappeti family has curated the finest hand-knotted rugs from the nomadic tribes and urban master-weavers of Herat and Persia. What began as a small stall in the Silk Road markets has evolved into a global symbol of textile artistry.

Each rug is more than a floor covering; it is a repository of history, featuring motifs passed down through centuries, representing the unique identity of the hands that tied each individual knot.

The Art of Hand-Knotting

A single Afghan Tappeti can take up to 14 months to complete. Our artisans use the traditional asymmetrical knotting technique, ensuring a level of detail and durability that machine-made replicas can never achieve.

Natural Pigments

We use only organic dyes derived from madder root, pomegranate rinds, and indigo—colors that age gracefully over decades.

The Loom

Every rug begins on a vertical loom, where the weaver translates mental maps into intricate physical patterns knot by knot.

Final Shearing

After weaving, the rug is sheared and washed in mountain spring water to bring out the natural luster of the wool.

Ethical Sourcing

Empowering Artisans

We believe in a transparent supply chain. Our weavers are partners, not just producers. We ensure fair living wages that are 40% above the local industry average, providing economic stability to over 500 weaving families in the Herat province.

100% Hand-Spun Wool
Zero Child Labor

Environmental Stewardship

Traditional methods are inherently sustainable. By using locally sourced Ghazni wool and plant-based dyes, we minimize our ecological footprint. These rugs are built to last for generations, the ultimate antithesis to fast furniture culture.
